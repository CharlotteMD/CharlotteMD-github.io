About Me

I’ve been searching for the thing that makes me look forward to going into work everyday. The more I code, the more it feels like development is the right path for me. It utilises my logical and intellectually curious brain, creativity, business acumen and commitment to hard work that I have developed so far in my career. I am naturally ambitious and driven and the small but frequent wins from debugging and passing tests really motivate my continued success. I’m really excited that the skills I have learnt are already allowing me to build my ideas into something physical. I am buzzing with ideas and would value working for an organisation that encourages innovation and creativity.


After some courses online, studying Web Development full-time seemed like the obvious next step. I see my decision to study at General Assembly as an investment in myself and my future. I aspire to work in an organisation who sees my potential and are willing to continue to develop me so that over time, I can continue to contribute to the organisation.

Projects

Whodunit

This was my first app, completed just 3 weeks after the start of the Web Development Immersive Course.

The brief was to create a game using what we had learnt so far: HTML 5, CSS3 and JavaScript.

I’m particularly proud of the use of HTML Audio so do have the sound on when you play!

GuestBook

This was my first full stack app and I’m proud of it as I can see couples using it!

We had to create a full stack app using Express.

My highlight of this project is how I managed to make each guestbook private by generating a secret code for each book.

In the future I would like to develop the guest book page to show the data in a variety of more exciting ways - graphs, polls etc.

As part of the course we had to build an app as part of a group.

With two other developers, we worked together to create a ski holiday planning app.

I loved working collaboratively on this project!

My main contribution was the destination pages, where I learnt to implement Google Maps and Google Places APIs.
