About Me

After taking some courses online and attending tech meetups, studying Web Development full-time seemed like the obvious next step. I see my decision to study at General Assembly as an investment in myself and my future. I’ve been searching for the thing that makes me look forward to going into work everyday, and the more I code, the more it feels like development is that thing. It utilises a lot of the skills that I have developed so far in my career: logic, intellectual curiosity, creativity and business acumen, and ultimately commitment to hard work. I am naturally ambitious and driven and the small but frequent wins from debugging and passing tests really motivate my continued success.


I’m really excited that the skills I have learnt are already allowing me to build my ideas into something physical. I am buzzing with ideas for my next side project and would value working for an organisation that encourages innovation and creativity. I aspire to work in an organisation who sees my potential and are willing to continue to develop me, so that over time, I can continue to contribute to the organisation.
